Data

We used two forms of data to build our models: text extracted from YouTube product review videos, and Amazon Reviews data. And for both, the focus was on products we believed would have a substantial amount of video product reviews. This included TV's, cell phones, and laptops. Products that are higher-ticket items which probably result in a person doing some homework prior to making a purchase.


Text from Product Review Videos

For keyword and sentence extraction, we used text extracted from actual product review videos. By manually scrutinizing the text output of actual produt review videos, we were better able to understand what our models needed to do. Six videos were used, 3 punctuated and 3 unpunctuated, for supervised learning.


Amazon Reviews Data

We did leverage some publicly available Amazon review data. Requested citations are noted below.

The written Amazon reviews were used as training data when creating the summarization model. Exploratory data analysis was conducted on 60GB+ of data. We ended up with a training dataset of approximately 500K data points. The data included: number of stars, review title, review text, and Verified/Not Verified.

Additional data cleaning included removing reviews where:

  • Summary field included the word 'star', or had only 1-2 words
  • Review text was less than four words
  • Review text was an actual HTML tag


    • The data is provided by Julian McAuley from the University of California, San Diego. His site can be found here: http://jmcauley.ucsd.edu/data/amazon/

    We specifically used data from the 2018 page and limited the review dates to 2015 - 2018. Cameras, electronics, and phone data were used.

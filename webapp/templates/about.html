Why Create TLDW; Too Long Didn't Watch?


As product review videos grow in popularity, one potential drawback is the time commitment. Wouldn't it be great if there were a way to get the highlights from a product review video without having to watch it? That's how Too Long Didn't Watch came to be.

When considering the audience for a solution like Too Long Didn't Watch, we felt two distinct groups would benefit. The first is consumers. Several key statistics below are indicative of trends around product review videos.

The second group are product and marketing managers. Our long-term goal is to allow business people to upload multiple videos at a time and provide highlights as seen in the demo but aggregated. Businesses can find out what influencers are saying about their product. And an aggregated view could provide the equivalent of focus group feedback. As part of our long-term strategy, we would like to conduct our own research with product and marketing managers to better understand what output would be most useful.

Take a look at some interesting statistics on product review videos:




As of 2019, online product reviews influence the purchase decisions for 93% of consumers.[1]


Reputation matters!


Amazon and Yelp have admitted a problem with fake reviews over the past 7 years leading both companies to alter algorithms and file lawsuits against sellers who post fake reviews.[2]



Behavioral changes between generations has influenced which categories of videos are most popular on YouTube. Among Millennials and Gen Z, product reviews and unboxing videos fall in the top three categories of watched content on YouTube.[3]

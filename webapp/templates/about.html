Why Create TLDW; Too Long Don't Watch?


As a relatively tech-savvy team, we've all found ourselves watching video product reviews prior to making a purchase. Whether interested in a specific person's thoughts, or wanting to hear the pros and cons from multiple sources, we've all spent countless hours watching review videos.

So wouldn't it be great if there were a way to get the highlights from a product review video without having to watch it?

We wondered if others would appreciate a tool like this and found quite a few interesting statistics. In the end, we believe Too Long Don't Watch is ideal for people who watch product review videos prior to making purchases, as well as product and marketing managers who want to see what the influencers are saying about their products but don't have time to watch (and log) tens, if not hundreds, of videos.

Here are some interesting statistics on product review products:




As of 2019, online product reviews influence the purchase decisions for 93% of consumers.[1]


Reputation matters!


Amazon and Yelp have admitted a problem with fake reviews over the past 7 years leading both companies to alter algorithms and file lawsuits against sellers who post fake reviews.[2]



Some claim that watching a person review a product in a video, see their face, and talk about it is easier to trust than anonymously posted comments.


And behavioral changes between generations has influenced which categories of videos are most popular on YouTube. Among Millennials and Gen Z, product reviews and unboxing videos fall in the top three categories of watched content on YouTube.[3]
